About


In the RNA base-pair steps database (RNASTEPS) you will find all steps found in "so-called" intact helical regions of RNA. These intact helical regions come about due to the capability of the 3DNA software package for distinguishing both continuous and quasi-continuous helical regions of nucleic acids. What is meant by continuous is that base-pairs stack in top of each other and connect through an intact sugar-phosphate backbone, whereas in the quasi-continuous case you will have stacked base-pairs whose sugar-phosphate backbone will be broken at some point. One typical example of this situation is the coaxial stacking of the anticodon and D stems of tRNA, which are identified by 3DNA as a quasicontinuous helix. To better illustrate intact helical regions of RNA they are shown in the following figure and denoted by H, along with other possible helical contexts which are not included currently in RNASTEPS but which will be developed in the future.



The data has been taken from the Protein Data Bank (PDB) for X-ray determined structures whose resolution is of 3.5 Angstroms or better. To better illustrate the order of a steps in our database in the following image an RNA base-pair step is depicted using the Leontis-Westhof annotation convention for nucleic acid base-pairs. A circle denotes a Watson-Crick edge, a square denotes a Hoogsteen edge, and a triangle denotes a Sugar edge. Open polygons mean that the glycosidic bonds are in the trans orientation and if there were filled polygons they would mean the cis orientation.
